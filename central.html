Ndop (portrait figure) of King Mishe miShyaang maMbul

ID

  • 1760-80
  • Kuba people
  • Democratic Republic of the Congo
  • Wood

History

  • Ndop means statue
  • Kuba artists were professional woodcarvers, blacksmiths, and weavers
  • Meant to represent King
  • Unrealistic
  • Celebrates the king's genorosity
  • Meant to be a record of his reign
  • Kuba refers to about 19 groups of people who follow the king (nyim)

Features

  • Calm face to represent dignity
  • Drum is used to represent King Mishe

Power figure (Nkisi n’kondi)

ID

  • Late 19th century
  • Wood and metal
  • Kongo Peoples
  • Democratic Republic of the Congo

History

  • Nkisi means spirit
  • contains sacred substances that are activated by supernatural forces
  • Magical charm
  • Can be used to resolve arguments or lawsuits, avenger, or guardian
  • Medicine often stored in head

Features

  • Figures activated by inserting nails, pegs, and sticks and each item reprsents something different

Female (pwo) mask

ID

  • Chokwe people
  • Angola
  • Wood, fiber, brass, pigment
  • 19th–early 20th century

History

  • Matrilineal society
  • Carved and worn by men
  • Honor young fertile women
  • Used for dance
  • Also wore wooden breasts
  • Connection to the spiritual world
  • Honors an ideal Chokwe woman who had successfully given birth

Features

  • White kaylin around eyes
  • Ideal woman
  • Ideal features: wide forehead, small nose, calm expression, healthy complexion
  • Tattoo around eyes

ID

  • Luba people
  • 19th - 20th century
  • Wood, beads, metal

History

  • Used to tell history
  • Read holding it with the left head and touching the right forefinger to board
  • Only trained people could read it
